Through their vigilant gaze, we can now see hotspots of emissions that pose serious threats to our climate.

    Every day, in a carefully orchestrated dance, satellites orbit the Earth, gathering immense volumes of data on atmospheric methane concentrations. Instruments like TROPOMI not only provide daily updates but also trace the footprints of emissions over vast geographical swathes. In the backdrop, newer technologies such as MethaneSAT emerge, promising to enhance the resolution and precision of these increasingly critical observations.

    ### Understanding the Science
    Yet, the tale of methane is not merely one of numbers. To manage these emissions, we must delve into their origins. From the heart of industrial sites belching methane to the quiet but potent emissions from agricultural sources, methane has many faces. Landfills teeming with organic waste and livestock grazing on pastures contribute significantly; even the **natural wetlands** offer their own share of emissions.

A quick cut to imagery highlights this ongoing saga: livestock in pastoral fields, flares at oil rigs, and the slow decomposition of organic materials at landfill sites, each playing a role in this complex story of atmospheric degradation.
